Source of Error in Detection of Entangled Photon Pairs within Parametric Down Converter (PDC) Crystal Sources

Status: In Progress

Parametric Down Converters (PDC) are a great source of entangled pairs of photons. In many situations, one manages to approximate the system as a single pair of photons emitted. In actuality, the system is in a superposition of all even numbers of photons (2, 4, 6, 8, etc.), which is due to our ignorance of the actual number of photon the crystal gives us. Because of conservation of energy and momentum, all we know for sure is that the crystal must give us pairs, but we have no way of knowing the exact amount before we measure the system. The assumption that the system is in the single pair photon state is sufficient comes from the extremely low probability that the crystal will even produce the two photon state at all. In most cases, the driving factor of pair production, nonlinearity of the crystal, is extremely small, so this assumption is valid. However, the neglect of higher order terms leads to an error in the probability of detection by detectors in a lab setting. Here, we attempt to calculate this discrepancy between the first order approximation and the higher order terms of the PDC state.

Complete Description of Fault Tolerant Quantum Gate Operations for Topological Majorana Qubit Systems

Status: Manuscript (Current Document )

Current qubit designs share a common problem of decoherence. This is a naturally occurring process which effectively destroys the quantum state of the system over time. Naturally, we want to limit nature's grip on our system, so that computation is possible for longer periods of time. Topological states have been proposed[2] as a possible method of creating robust or even fault tolerant qubits. An example of such a system is depicted in the cartoon on the left. As it turns out, the gate operations for this system look just like braids of rope (depicted in lower image), and, to perform computations, one simply moves the vortices around the physical area generating rotations of the qubit state[3][4].

In this work, we calculate the matrix representation for Majorana braids and then generalize the gates that are possible for any number of qubit and any number of Majorana.
